# 🏥 DAY 1 – FUNDAMENTALS: NURSING CONCEPTS (DETAILED)

---

# 🧠 1. WHAT IS NURSING?

### 📌 Definition (Exam-Oriented)

Nursing is a **profession focused on promoting, maintaining, and restoring health**, preventing illness, and caring for individuals, families, and communities.

👉 It includes:

* Physical care
* Emotional support
* Health education
* Rehabilitation

---

# 🎯 2. AIMS OF NURSING

* Promote health
* Prevent disease
* Restore health
* Relieve suffering
* Provide end-of-life care

---

# 🧩 3. METAPARADIGM OF NURSING (VERY IMPORTANT ⭐)

This is a **favorite NORCET question area**

### 🔑 4 Core Components:

### 1. Person

* Individual, family, or community receiving care
* Has physical, emotional, social, spiritual needs

---

### 2. Health

* State of **complete physical, mental, and social well-being**
* Not just absence of disease

---

### 3. Environment

* Internal + external surroundings affecting health
* Includes:

    * Physical (cleanliness, noise)
    * Social (family, culture)

---

### 4. Nursing

* Actions taken to care for the patient
* Includes:

    * Assessment
    * Care
    * Support

---

# 🧠 4. CHARACTERISTICS OF NURSING

* Scientific (based on knowledge)
* Art (skill + compassion)
* Holistic (whole patient care)
* Dynamic (changes with needs)
* Patient-centered

---

# 🏗️ 5. NURSING PROCESS (HIGHLY IMPORTANT ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐)

👉 This is **asked repeatedly in exams**

## 🔄 5 Steps:

### 1. Assessment

* Collect patient data
* Example: BP, symptoms

---

### 2. Diagnosis

* Identify patient problems
* Example: “Impaired breathing”

---

### 3. Planning

* Set goals
* Decide interventions

---

### 4. Implementation

* Perform nursing actions

---

### 5. Evaluation

* Check if goals are achieved

---

## 🧠 Shortcut:

👉 **ADPIE**

* A – Assessment
* D – Diagnosis
* P – Planning
* I – Implementation
* E – Evaluation

---

# ⚖️ 6. PROFESSIONAL ROLES OF A NURSE

* Caregiver
* Advocate (protect patient rights)
* Educator
* Counselor
* Manager
* Researcher

---

# 🧑‍⚕️ 7. QUALITIES OF A GOOD NURSE

* Compassion
* Communication skills
* Critical thinking
* Patience
* Responsibility

---

# 🧾 8. TYPES OF NURSING

* Clinical nursing
* Community health nursing
* Pediatric nursing
* Psychiatric nursing
* Geriatric nursing

---

# 🧭 9. HOLISTIC CARE (VERY IMPORTANT)

👉 Treat the patient as a **whole person**, not just disease.

Includes:

* Physical
* Emotional
* Social
* Spiritual care

---

# ⚖️ 10. ETHICAL PRINCIPLES IN NURSING (IMPORTANT)

### 🔑 Core Principles:

* Autonomy → Patient has right to decide
* Beneficence → Do good
* Non-maleficence → Do no harm
* Justice → Fair treatment
* Confidentiality → Protect patient info

---

# 🚨 11. LEGAL ASPECTS (BASIC)

* Informed consent
* Negligence
* Malpractice
* Documentation importance

# **Q1. Define Nursing. Explain the concepts and characteristics of Nursing.**

## **Answer:**

### **Definition:**

Nursing is a profession focused on **promotion, prevention, restoration of health, and care of individuals, families, and communities**.

---

## **Concepts of Nursing:**

### **1. Person**

* Individual receiving care
* Includes physical, psychological, social, spiritual aspects

---

### **2. Environment**

* Surroundings affecting health
* Internal & external factors

---

### **3. Health**

* State of complete physical, mental, social well-being

---

### **4. Nursing**

* Actions taken to improve patient health

---

## **Characteristics of Nursing:**

* Scientific and evidence-based
* Holistic approach
* Patient-centered
* Goal-oriented
* Dynamic and evolving

---

### **Conclusion:**

Nursing integrates **science and compassion** to provide comprehensive care.

---

# **Q2. Explain Nursing Process in detail.**

## **Answer:**

### **Definition:**

A systematic method used by nurses to **plan and provide patient care**.

---

## **Steps of Nursing Process:**

### **1. Assessment**

* Collect data (subjective + objective)

---

### **2. Diagnosis**

* Identify patient problems
* Example: impaired breathing

---

### **3. Planning**

* Set goals (SMART)
* Develop interventions

---

### **4. Implementation**

* Carry out interventions

---

### **5. Evaluation**

* Check if goals achieved

---

## **Importance:**

* Ensures systematic care
* Improves patient outcomes
* Enhances critical thinking

---

## **Answer:**

### **Definition:**

Holistic care addresses **whole person**, not just disease.

## **Components:**

* Social

## **Examples:**
