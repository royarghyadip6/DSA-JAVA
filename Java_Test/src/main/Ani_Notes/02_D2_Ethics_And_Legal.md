# ⚖️ 1. NURSING ETHICS (CORE CONCEPT)

### 📌 Definition

Ethics are **moral principles that guide nursing behavior and decision-making** in patient care.

👉 In simple terms:
**“What is right or wrong in nursing practice.”**

---

# 🧭 2. PRINCIPLES OF ETHICS (VERY IMPORTANT ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐)

These are **frequently asked in NORCET (especially scenario-based)**

---

## 🔑 1. Autonomy

👉 Patient’s right to make their own decisions

**Example:**

* Patient refuses surgery → Nurse must respect it

---

## ❤️ 2. Beneficence

👉 Do good for the patient

**Example:**

* Giving best treatment and care

---

## 🚫 3. Non-Maleficence

👉 Do no harm

**Example:**

* Avoid wrong medication

---

## ⚖️ 4. Justice

👉 Fair and equal treatment

**Example:**

* No discrimination (rich/poor, caste, gender)

---

## 🔒 5. Confidentiality

👉 Protect patient information

**Example:**

* Not sharing medical details unnecessarily

---

## 🤝 6. Fidelity

👉 Being faithful and keeping promises

**Example:**

* Providing care as promised

---

## 🗣️ 7. Veracity

👉 Telling the truth

**Example:**

* Informing patient honestly

---

# 🧠 QUICK MEMORY TIP:

👉 **A B N J C F V**
(Autonomy, Beneficence, Non-maleficence, Justice, Confidentiality, Fidelity, Veracity)

---

# 🧾 3. CODE OF ETHICS FOR NURSES

* Respect human dignity
* Maintain confidentiality
* Provide safe care
* Be accountable
* Maintain professional competence

---

# ⚖️ 4. LEGAL ASPECTS IN NURSING (VERY IMPORTANT ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐)

👉 These questions are often **direct in NORCET**

---

## 📜 A. INFORMED CONSENT

### 📌 Definition:

Permission given by patient **after full explanation**

### ✅ Must include:

* Procedure details
* Risks
* Benefits
* Alternatives

### ❗ Important:

* Must be **voluntary**
* Patient must be **competent**

---

## ⚠️ B. NEGLIGENCE

### 📌 Definition:

Failure to provide proper care → causes harm

**Example:**

* Not monitoring patient properly

---

## 🚨 C. MALPRACTICE

### 📌 Definition:

Professional negligence by a nurse

**Example:**

* Giving wrong injection

---

## 📄 D. DOCUMENTATION

👉 “If not documented, it is not done”

### Importance:

* Legal evidence
* Communication
* Continuity of care

---

## 🔐 E. CONFIDENTIALITY (LEGAL SIDE)

* Patient data must be protected
* Can only be shared when:

    * Required by law
    * Patient consent

---

## ⚖️ F. ASSAULT & BATTERY

### Assault:

* Threatening patient

### Battery:

* Touching without consent

**Example:**

* Giving injection without permission

---

## 🧾 G. LICENSURE

* Nurse must be registered
* Practice legally

---

# ⚠️ 5. COMMON LEGAL ISSUES IN NURSING

* Medication errors
* Patient falls
* Wrong procedures
* Lack of consent
* Poor documentation

---

# 🧠 6. ETHICAL VS LEGAL (IMPORTANT DIFFERENCE)

| Ethics                | Legal               |
|-----------------------|---------------------|
| Moral principles      | Law rules           |
| Based on values       | Based on government |
| Not always punishable | Punishable          |

# 🧪 NORCET MCQs – ETHICS & LEGAL (HIGH LEVEL)

---

## **Q1. A patient refuses life-saving treatment. The nurse should:**

A. Inform relatives and proceed
B. Respect the patient’s decision
C. Ignore refusal
D. Force treatment

✅ **Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
Autonomy → patient has right to decide, even if decision is risky.

---

## **Q2. Giving medication without patient consent is:**

A. Assault
B. Battery
C. Negligence
D. Malpractice

✅ **Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
Battery = physical contact without consent.

---

## **Q3. A nurse threatens a patient to take medicine. This is:**

A. Assault
B. Battery
C. Negligence
D. Beneficence

✅ **Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
Assault = threat without touching.

---

## **Q4. Which situation best represents beneficence?**

A. Respecting refusal
B. Giving best possible care
C. Maintaining secrecy
D. Equal treatment

✅ **Answer: B**

---

## **Q5. Which is NOT required for informed consent?**

A. Voluntary decision
B. Full explanation
C. Family approval mandatory
D. Patient competence

✅ **Answer: C**

**Explanation:**
Family approval is not mandatory if patient is competent.

## **Q6. A nurse forgets to raise side rails and patient falls. This is:**

A. Assault
B. Malpractice

D. Battery

✅ **Answer: C**

A. Assault
