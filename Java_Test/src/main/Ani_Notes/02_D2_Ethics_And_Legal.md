# ⚖️ 1. NURSING ETHICS (CORE CONCEPT)

### 📌 Definition

Ethics are **moral principles that guide nursing behavior and decision-making** in patient care.

👉 In simple terms:
**“What is right or wrong in nursing practice.”**

---

# 🧭 2. PRINCIPLES OF ETHICS (VERY IMPORTANT ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐)

These are **frequently asked in NORCET (especially scenario-based)**

---

## 🔑 1. Autonomy

👉 Patient’s right to make their own decisions

**Example:**

* Patient refuses surgery → Nurse must respect it

---

## ❤️ 2. Beneficence

👉 Do good for the patient

**Example:**

* Giving the best treatment and care

---

## 🚫 3. Non-Maleficence

👉 Do no harm

**Example:**

* Avoid wrong medication

---

## ⚖️ 4. Justice

👉 Fair and equal treatment

**Example:**

* No discrimination (rich/poor, caste, gender)

---

## 🔒 5. Confidentiality

👉 Protect patient information

**Example:**

* Not sharing medical details unnecessarily

---

## 🤝 6. Fidelity

👉 Being faithful and keeping promises

**Example:**

* Providing care as promised

---

## 🗣️ 7. Veracity

👉 Telling the truth

**Example:**

* Informing patient honestly

---

# 🧠 QUICK MEMORY TIP:

👉 **A B N J C F V**
(Autonomy, Beneficence, Non-maleficence, Justice, Confidentiality, Fidelity, Veracity)

---

# 🧾 3. CODE OF ETHICS FOR NURSES

* Respect human dignity
* Maintain confidentiality
* Provide safe care
* Be accountable
* Maintain professional competence

---

# ⚖️ 4. LEGAL ASPECTS IN NURSING (VERY IMPORTANT ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐)

👉 These questions are often **direct in NORCET**

---

## 📜 A. INFORMED CONSENT

### 📌 Definition:

Permission given by patient **after full explanation**

### ✅ Must include:

* Procedure details
* Risks
* Benefits
* Alternatives

### ❗ Important:

* Must be **voluntary**
* Patient must be **competent**

---

## ⚠️ B. NEGLIGENCE

### 📌 Definition:

Failure to provide proper care → causes harm

**Example:**

* Not monitoring patient properly

---

## 🚨 C. MALPRACTICE

### 📌 Definition:

Professional negligence by a nurse

**Example:**

* Giving wrong injection

---

## 📄 D. DOCUMENTATION

👉 “If not documented, it is not done”

### Importance:

* Legal evidence
* Communication
* Continuity of care

---

## 🔐 E. CONFIDENTIALITY (LEGAL SIDE)

* Patient data must be protected
* Can only be shared when:

    * Required by law
    * Patient consent

---

## ⚖️ F. ASSAULT & BATTERY

### Assault:

* Threatening patient

### Battery:

* Touching without consent

**Example:**

* Giving injection without permission

---

## 🧾 G. LICENSURE

* Nurse must be registered
* Practice legally

---

# ⚠️ 5. COMMON LEGAL ISSUES IN NURSING

* Medication errors
* Patient falls
* Wrong procedures
* Lack of consent
* Poor documentation

---

# 🧠 6. ETHICAL VS LEGAL (IMPORTANT DIFFERENCE)

| Ethics                | Legal               |
|-----------------------|---------------------|
| Moral principles      | Law rules           |
| Based on values       | Based on government |
| Not always punishable | Punishable          |

# **Q1. Define Nursing Ethics. Explain principles of Nursing Ethics with examples.**

## **Answer:**

### **Definition:**

Nursing ethics refers to the **moral principles that guide nursing practice**, ensuring safe, respectful, and professional care.

---

## **Principles of Nursing Ethics:**

### **1. Autonomy**

* Respect patient’s right to make decisions
* Example: Patient refusing treatment

---

### **2. Beneficence**

* Act in patient’s best interest
* Example: Providing timely care

---

### **3. Non-Maleficence**

* “Do no harm”
* Example: Avoiding medication errors

---

### **4. Justice**

* Equal and fair treatment
* Example: No discrimination

---

### **5. Veracity**

* Truthfulness
* Example: Giving correct information

---

### **6. Fidelity**

* Keeping promises
* Example: Returning at promised time

---

### **Conclusion:**

Ethical principles ensure **safe, respectful, and patient-centered care**.

---

# **Q2. Explain informed consent in detail.**

## **Answer:**

### **Definition:**

Informed consent is a **legal process where a patient agrees to treatment after understanding risks, benefits, and alternatives**.

---

## **Elements of Valid Consent:**

1. **Competence** – Patient must be mentally sound
2. **Disclosure** – Complete information provided
3. **Understanding** – Patient comprehends information
4. **Voluntariness** – No pressure
5. **Consent** – Agreement given

---

## **Types of Consent:**

* Implied consent (routine care)
* Expressed consent (verbal/written)
* Informed consent (detailed)

---

## **Role of Nurse:**

* Witness consent
* Ensure patient understanding
* Clarify doubts
* Report issues

---

## **Importance:**

* Protects patient rights
* Prevents legal issues

---

# **Q3. Define negligence and malpractice. Explain with examples.**

## **Answer:**

Failure to provide standard care.

👉 Example: Not monitoring patient

---

### **Malpractice:**

Professional negligence causing harm.

👉 Example: Giving wrong medication dose

---

## **Elements of Negligence:**

1. Duty of care
2. Breach of duty
3. Damage
4. Causation

---

## **Prevention:**

* Follow protocols
* Proper documentation
* Patient safety practices
